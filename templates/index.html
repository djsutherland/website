Prospective students: Like most North American schools, we only accept applications through the departmental process, with a deadline in December. There is no reason to contact me about admissions at this time of year, and I will probably not reply.

#}

I am considering applications this year, but expect to take somewhere between zero and two new students this time depending on several factors. You should feel free to just apply to the department and indicate your interest in working with me in the application; there is no need to email me, and I will likely not reply due to the volume of emails.

Note that the split between master's and PhD programs is different in Canada from the US; I am unlikely to seriously consider PhD applicants who don't already have significant research experience in machine learning or statistics (ideally, something close to my areas of interest), but will certainly look at promising master's candidates without that.

Machine learning graduate school programs are currently extremely competitive, since there is very high interest relative to the number of potential advisors. Make sure you apply to more than one program.

Here is a great Twitter thread overviewing lots of options for getting feedback on your application materials, including ones by various departments, for different affinity groups, and also one for those interested in learning theory. I'm involved with the Queer in AI program, which additionally reimburses some application fees for queer students; there are also programs from Black in AI, LatinX in AI, and the Deep Learning Indaba (for students from Africa). Also note that UBC waives its fee for applicants from the UN's list of 50 least developed countries, including Bangladesh, Ethiopia, Nepal, and Uganda.

Anyone with specific research connections / questions / etc should feel free to get in touch at any time, via email / Twitter DM / whatever.

Trans and gender-expansive or other queer people, also please reach out whenever, about specific things or just saying hi. Consider using my personal email (the djsutherland.ml one) for privacy reasons; Twitter DMs or Queer in AI's Slack are also good.

My current research interests include:

  • Learning and testing on sets and distributions: two-sample tests, evaluating and training implicit generative models (e.g. GANs), density estimation, distribution regression.
  • Learning “deep kernels,” and representation learning more broadly.
  • Statistical learning theory in general.

I was previously a Research Assistant Professor at TTIC, did a postdoc with Arthur Gretton at the Gatsby Computational Neuroscience Unit, University College London, and I did my Ph.D. at Carnegie Mellon University with Jeff Schneider.
